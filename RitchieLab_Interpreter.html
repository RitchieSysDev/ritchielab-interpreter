Clinical Correlation Notes

  • Always correlate laboratory findings with the patient’s clinical presentation and history.
  • Abnormal results should be repeated to confirm before initiating extensive workup.
  • Peripheral blood film examination is recommended when indices are abnormal or when clinical suspicion is high.

    Produces: Low TSH, High FT4, High FT3 — a false Graves pattern.

    • hCG stimulates thyroid → ↓ TSH in first trimester

    • Normal or low TSH
    • ↑ Reverse T3

    Red Flags Requiring Urgent Action
    Urgent
    • Thyroid storm — markedly elevated FT4/FT3 with severe systemic illness

    High-Yield Diagnostic Pearls

    Factor VII Sensitivity

    PT is more sensitive to factor VII deficiency because factor VII has the shortest half-life among the vitamin K-dependent factors.

    Factor XII Paradox

    aPTT is prolonged by deficiencies of factors VIII, IX, XI, and XII; factor XII deficiency does NOT cause bleeding despite a prolonged aPTT.

    Normal Screen Does Not Exclude Bleeding

    A normal PT and aPTT do not exclude a bleeding disorder; platelet dysfunction, von Willebrand disease, and factor XIII deficiency may all have normal screening tests.

    Lupus Anticoagulant Paradox

    Lupus anticoagulant paradoxically prolongs aPTT but is associated with thrombosis rather than bleeding.

    DIC Classic Pentad

    In DIC, the characteristic combination is: prolonged PT and aPTT, thrombocytopenia, low fibrinogen, elevated D-dimer, and often schistocytes on a blood film.

    Liver Disease Factor VIII

    Severe liver disease reduces synthesis of most coagulation factors (except factor VIII, which is often normal or increased), leading to prolonged PT and, later, prolonged aPTT.

    Mixing Study Interpretation

    A mixing study that corrects suggests a factor deficiency; failure to correct suggests a circulating inhibitor (e.g., lupus anticoagulant or a specific factor inhibitor).

    Warfarin Target Ranges

    Target INR: DVT/PE (2-3), Atrial fibrillation (2-3), Mechanical mitral valve (2.5-3.5). Always confirm the indication before adjusting therapy.

    D-Dimer Exclusion Rule

    Normal D-dimer effectively excludes VTE in appropriately selected low-risk patients. Do not rely on D-dimer alone in high-risk or post-operative patients.

    vWD and Factor VIII

    In von Willebrand disease, Factor VIII levels may be low because vWF carries Factor VIII. This can cause both prolonged aPTT and bleeding.

    Adults (19 - 64 years)

    Interpret according to risk factors: diabetes, hypertension, CKD, pregnancy, autoimmune disease.

    Proteinuria

    Possible causes: Diabetic nephropathy, nephrotic syndrome, hypertension, CKD

    Follow-up: UPCR/ACR, serum creatinine, eGFR, renal ultrasound

    Hematuria

    Possible causes: Stones, tumors, infection, trauma, glomerulonephritis

    Follow-up: Repeat UA, microscopy, culture, renal/bladder ultrasound, CT urography, cystoscopy

    Glucosuria

    Possible causes: Diabetes mellitus, pregnancy, Fanconi syndrome

    Follow-up: Fasting plasma glucose, HbA1c, OGTT

    Ketones

    Seen in: Diabetic ketoacidosis, starvation, vomiting, low-carbohydrate diets

    Follow-up: Blood glucose, serum ketones, ABG, electrolytes

    Pregnancy

    Physiological Changes: Mild protein (<300 mg/day) may occur. Increased urinary frequency. Higher risk of asymptomatic bacteriuria.

    FindingClinical ConcernAction
    ProteinuriaPreeclampsiaBP check, edema assessment, LFTs, platelets
    NitritesUTICulture, treat to prevent pyelonephritis
    KetonesHyperemesis gravidarumElectrolytes, hydration status
    GlucoseGestational diabetesOGTT, glucose monitoring
    Always correlate proteinuria with: Blood pressure, edema, and gestational age

    Older Adults (>= 65 years)

    Common Normal Changes: Lower concentrating ability, slightly dilute urine, increased asymptomatic bacteriuria.

    Interpret Carefully: Positive leukocytes without symptoms may not require antibiotics.

    Pattern Recognition

    Common urinalysis patterns and their likely diagnoses

    Protein +RBC Casts
    Glomerulonephritis
    Dysmorphic RBCs, hematuria with proteinuria. Consider post-streptococcal, IgA, lupus.
    Protein +++Fatty Casts
    Nephrotic Syndrome
    Heavy proteinuria, edema, hyperlipidemia. Minimal change disease in children.
    WBC +Nitrites +Bacteria
    Urinary Tract Infection
    Pyuria with bacteriuria. Obtain culture and sensitivity.
    WBC CastsFever
    Pyelonephritis
    WBC casts with flank pain and fever. Requires antibiotics and imaging.
    Blood +Crystals
    Kidney Stones
    Hematuria with calcium oxalate or uric acid crystals. Renal colic history.
    Glucose +++Ketones +
    Diabetes Mellitus / DKA
    Glucosuria with ketonuria. Check blood glucose, ABG, electrolytes.
    Bilirubin +Dark Urine
    Liver Disease
    Conjugated hyperbilirubinemia. Check LFTs, hepatitis serology, abdominal US.
    IsosthenuriaCasts
    Chronic Kidney Disease
    Fixed SG ~1.010 with broad/waxy casts. Check eGFR, renal ultrasound.

    Recommended Follow-Up Tests

    Investigations to consider based on urinalysis findings

    Persistent Proteinuria

    Urine protein-to-creatinine ratio (UPCR) or albumin-to-creatinine ratio (ACR), serum creatinine, eGFR, renal ultrasound

    Routine

    Hematuria

    Repeat urinalysis, urine microscopy, urine culture, renal/bladder ultrasound, CT urography (if indicated), cystoscopy in adults

    Urgent

    Suspected UTI

    Urine culture and antimicrobial susceptibility testing

    Urgent

    Glucosuria

    Fasting plasma glucose, HbA1c, oral glucose tolerance test (when indicated)

    Routine

    Ketonuria

    Blood glucose, serum ketones, arterial/venous blood gas, electrolytes

    Urgent

    Bilirubinuria

    Liver function tests, hepatitis serology (if indicated), abdominal ultrasound

    Urgent

    Crystalluria

    Stone analysis (if available), serum calcium, phosphate, uric acid, PTH (when appropriate), 24-hour urine metabolic evaluation

    Consider

    Casts Suggesting Renal Disease

    Kidney function tests, complement levels (C3/C4), ANA, ANCA, anti-GBM antibodies, serum protein electrophoresis, nephrology referral

    Urgent

    Persistent Sterile Pyuria

    STI testing, tuberculosis evaluation (if risk factors), imaging for stones or obstruction, assessment for interstitial nephritis
